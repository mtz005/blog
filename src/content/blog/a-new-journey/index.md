For the past couple of weeks I've been working on something new - and it's the most fun I've had professionally for as long as I can remember. You know something is truly engaging when you find yourself with a dumb grin on your face 😁.

The context for how I got here: after almost 20 years at [Code11](https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:activity:7455287862306144256/), it was time for a change. I started by looking for another job, then realized a few months off would be a better call. In the back of my head I'd been craving something else - building my own product. I'd wanted to do it for a long time; I just never had the right context. Well, now I did: more time, and AI making it genuinely easier to build than ever before. All I needed was a solid idea. As luck would have it, one would present itself.

I won't detail what the product is about just yet, but I can give some small teasers:

|              |                                                          |
| ------------ | -------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Audience** | B2C                                                      |
| **Reach**    | Local → regional → global                                |
| **Platform** | Primarily mobile                                         |
| **Model**    | Freemium                                                 |
| **Vibe**     | Delight, not pain relief                                 |
| **Team**     | Just me building the product + business partner on sales |
| **Stage**    | MVP in progress                                          |

Here's my honest read on where I stand:

|                                 |            |
| ------------------------------- | ---------- |
| The product will be great       | ██████████ |
| There's a real desire for this  | ████████░░ |
| People will actually pay for it | ███████░░░ |
| I can build and ship it solo    | █████████░ |
| It can reach a large audience   | █████░░░░░ |

The biggest risk I see is that it will fail to reach a wide audience, despite the product itself being great. Even so, there are some cards we can play when the time for that will come, so at the very least we won't go down without a fight.

What I will detail now though, is that I intend to build this product in public. I will document my journey from idea to concept, mocks to MVP, and launch to full release.

So, this is what I'm working on at the moment, and fairly soon I will begin this series of posts, documenting our entire experience. All of the posts will be available on LinkedIn, though a more in-depth version of each post (going into more technical details, with more supporting materials) will be available here on my blog.

The first real post - going into what the app actually is - drops once we hit open beta in early June. See you then.
